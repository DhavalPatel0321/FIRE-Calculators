# Safe withdrawal rates

The safe withdrawal rate is the percentage of a portfolio used to estimate sustainable first-year retirement spending. In the planner, it is the denominator in the core FIRE target:

`fireNumber = annualExpenses / safeWithdrawalRate`

The 4% default is the industry-standard starting point, but SWR is one of the most consequential assumptions in FIRE planning. A one percentage point shift changes the target portfolio by roughly 25%.

## Common SWR choices

| SWR | Implied portfolio multiple | When this value makes sense | Source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 3.0% | 33.3x expenses | Very conservative. Appropriate for 50+ year horizons, low-return environments, or users who want near-certainty of not running out. | Common safety-margin guidance for young FIRE seekers |
| 3.3% | 30.3x expenses | Often cited for early retirees with 40+ year horizons. | ERN Safe Withdrawal Rate series |
| 3.5% | 28.6x expenses | Morningstar's 2026 base case for a 30-year retirement with a 30-50% equity allocation. | Morningstar 2026 SWR study |
| 3.9% | 25.6x expenses | Morningstar's 2026 base case for a stock-heavy 30-year retirement. | Morningstar 2026 SWR study |
| 4.0% | 25.0x expenses | Default. Trinity Study / Bengen 1994, historically successful for 30-year retirements with a balanced allocation. | Trinity Study 1998 |
| 4.5% | 22.2x expenses | Upper end of reasonable; generally requires flexible withdrawals. | Bengen later research |
| 4.7% | 21.3x expenses | Bengen's updated research suggests this was historically safe for 30-year horizons with a broader asset mix. | Bengen updated |

## Why v1 uses one SWR

v1 uses the same safe withdrawal rate input for Traditional, Coast, Barista, Lean, and Fat FIRE. That keeps every result tied to one visible assumption and makes scenario comparison easier.

Users targeting very long retirements, unusually high spending, or a lower-risk portfolio can manually lower the SWR slider in <Link href="/plan">the planner</Link>. Per-variant SWR defaults and horizon-aware suggestions are planned for a later version, not v1.
